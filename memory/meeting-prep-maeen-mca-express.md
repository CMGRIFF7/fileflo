## SECTION 2 — Discovery Script

**Goal:** Understand their current process before showing anything. Listen for pain, don't pitch yet.

**Opening:**
> "Before I show you anything, I want to make sure I'm showing you what's actually relevant to you — can I ask a few quick questions about how you're handling compliance today?"

**The questions:**

**Q1 — Who owns it?**
> "Walk me through how you handle driver qualification files right now. Who owns that — is that you, a dispatcher, someone else?"

*Listen for: sole owner doing it themselves = high pain, no bandwidth. Dispatcher doing it = they need a system, not a person.*

**Q2 — How do you catch renewals?**
> "With 15 drivers, what happens when a CDL or medical card is about to expire — how do you actually catch that before it becomes a problem?"

*Listen for: "we check manually," "we have a spreadsheet," "honestly it's hit or miss." Any of these = perfect FileFlo setup.*

**Q3 — Roadside inspections**
> "Have you had DOT roadside inspections recently? How did those go?"

*Listen for: OOS events, citations, close calls. If they mention a vehicle being put OOS, that's a $16k+ cost story.*

**Q4 — The OOS rate (use carefully — don't lead with it)**
> "Is your vehicle inspection pass rate something you're tracking actively, or does it tend to catch you by surprise?"

*Use this after Q3 if they've opened up. Don't use it if they're defensive.*

**Q5 — The real fear**
> "If there was one compliance thing that keeps you up at night — what would it be?"

*This is your money question. Whatever they say, FileFlo probably solves it. Let them say it out loud.*

**Q6 — Buying readiness**
> "Are you actively looking for a solution right now, or is this more of a 'good to know' conversation?"

*Direct. Sets expectations for the close.*
